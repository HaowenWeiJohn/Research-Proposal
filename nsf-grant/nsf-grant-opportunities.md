### 1. Smart Health and Biomedical Research in the Era of AI (SCH)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Code** | NSF 25-542 |
| **Directorate** | CISE + NIH (joint program, 24 participating NIH institutes including NIBIB, NIA, NINDS, NIMH) |
| **Award** | Up to $1,200,000 total ($300K/year for up to 4 years) |
| **Deadline** | October 3, 2025 |
| **URL** | https://www.nsf.gov/funding/opportunities/sch-smart-health-biomedical-research-era-artificial-intelligence/nsf25-542/solicitation |

**Why it fits:** Arguably the single best target. The solicitation explicitly lists six research themes, three of which directly map to this work: (1) "Multimodal and reconfigurable sensing systems" with diverse sensor modalities including acoustic (ultrasound); (2) "Cyber-Physical Systems" for closed-loop health automation; (3) "Robotics" for health enhancement and disability reduction. Specifically calls out "wearable and implantable" applications, "integration of heterogeneous sensing modalities," and "multimodal data fusion." UltraPose, the multimodal reasoning benchmark, and the rehabilitation AI system hit three themes simultaneously.
