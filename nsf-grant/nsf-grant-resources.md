## Important Notes

### Submission System
- **FastLane is retired** — use **Research.gov** for all proposal preparation and submission.

### PAPPG Overview
The Proposal & Award Policies & Procedures Guide (PAPPG) is the authoritative document governing all NSF proposals. It includes:
- **Part I: Grant Proposal Guide (GPG)** — proposal preparation and submission instructions
- **Part II: Award & Administration Guide (AAG)** — post-award requirements

### Proposal Structure (per PAPPG Ch.2)
- **Cover Sheet**: start date, duration, organization info, applicable checkboxes (human subjects, international activities, etc.)
- **Project Summary**: 1 page max — overview, Intellectual Merit statement, Broader Impacts statement; must be understandable to readers outside the field
- **Table of Contents**: auto-generated by Research.gov
- **Project Description**: 15 pages max — includes objectives, significance, relation to current knowledge, plan of activities, methods, and a separate "Broader Impacts" section. URLs prohibited. Results from Prior NSF Support required if any PI/co-PI had NSF funding in past 5 years (up to 5 pages within the 15-page limit)
- **References Cited**: complete citations (all authors, title, volume, pages, year); no page limit but bibliographic citations only
- **Budget and Budget Justification**: justification limited to 5 pages per proposal (5 pages per subaward for collaborative)
- **Facilities, Equipment, and Other Resources**: describe organizational resources; include letters of collaboration for unfunded collaborations
- **Biographical Sketch(es)**: required for senior/key personnel; use SciENcv; must certify accuracy
- **Current and Pending Support**: required for all senior/key personnel; must be updated prior to award
- **Collaborators and Other Affiliations**: required for senior/key personnel
- **Data Management and Sharing Plan**: required
- **Letters of Collaboration**: document significant collaborative arrangements

### Format Requirements (per PAPPG Ch.2)
- **Fonts**: Arial, Courier New, or Palatino Linotype at 10pt+; Times New Roman or Computer Modern at 11pt+
- **Spacing**: no more than 6 lines per vertical inch
- **Margins**: minimum 1 inch on all sides
- **Paper**: standard 8.5" x 11"

### Budget Categories
- **Salaries**: PI salary limited to no more than 2 months from all NSF grants combined per year
- **Fringe Benefits**: calculated by applying fringe benefit rate to salaries
- **Equipment**: per-unit cost ≥ $5,000 with useful life > 1 year; must be individually listed and justified
- **Travel**: itemized by destination and cost; economy airfares on U.S.-flag carriers; dependent care for conference travel is allowable
- **Participant Support**: stipends, travel, registration for non-employees; indirect costs usually not allowed
- **Other Direct Costs**: materials/supplies, publication costs, consultant services, computer services, subawards
- **Indirect Costs (F&A)**: use Federally negotiated rate; 10% de minimis if no negotiated rate

### Unallowable Costs
- Entertainment and associated costs
- Meals and coffee breaks for intramural meetings
- Alcoholic beverages

### Special Proposal Types
- **RAPID**: time-sensitive research requiring expedited review
- **EAGER**: early-concept exploratory research with transformative potential
- **RAISE**: interdisciplinary research
- **GOALI**: academic–industry liaison
- **Conference**: support for conferences, workshops, symposia
- **Career Life Balance (CLB)**: supplemental funding for researchers with caregiving responsibilities

### Merit Review Criteria (weighted equally)
1. **Intellectual Merit** — The potential to advance knowledge within or across fields.
2. **Broader Impacts** — The potential to benefit society and contribute to desired societal outcomes.

Additional review elements: qualifications of PI/team, creativity/novelty, feasibility of plan, adequacy of resources.

### Common Deadlines
- **CAREER**: typically 4th Wednesday in July
- **GRFP**: typically mid-October (varies by field)
- Many core programs accept proposals **anytime** — check individual solicitations
