# NSF Grant Research: Comprehensive Guide to Grant Types and Options

## Context

This research was conducted to provide a comprehensive overview of NSF (National Science Foundation) grant types, funding mechanisms, and programs. The user (Haowen Wei) is a researcher at MIT's Device Realization Lab & Immersion Lab working on a wearable ultrasound research proposal focused on understanding human physical intelligence. This guide covers all major NSF funding pathways with relevance notes for the user's research area.

---

## 1. NSF Award Mechanisms (How Funds Are Disbursed)

| Mechanism | Description | Duration |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **Standard Grant** | Fixed amount for a fixed period; no implied future support | Varies (typically 1-5 years) |
| **Continuing Grant** | Funded incrementally (usually yearly); future increments contingent on progress & funds | Multi-year |
| **Cooperative Agreement (CA)** | Like a grant but with substantial NSF involvement in project activities | Multi-year |
| **Fellowship** | Awarded to individuals (not institutions) for training/research | Varies |
| **Supplement** | Additional funding added to an existing award (e.g., REU supplement) | Added to existing award period |

## 2. NSF Proposal Types (Special Mechanisms)

### 2.1 Research-Focused Special Types

| Type | Description | Max Funding | Max Duration | Key Feature |
|------|-------------|-------------|--------------|-------------|
| **CAREER** | Most prestigious early-career award; integrates research + education | Min $400K ($500K for BIO/ENG/Polar) | 5 years | Tenure-track assistant professors only |
| **EAGER** | High-risk, high-reward exploratory research | $300K | 2 years | Must contact program officer first; internal review (faster) |
| **RAPID** | Urgent data collection (disasters, ephemeral events) | $200K | 1 year | Internal review (faster); time-sensitive |
| **RAISE** | Bold interdisciplinary research spanning 2+ disciplines | $1M | 5 years | Must cross disciplinary boundaries |
| **GOALI** | Academic-industry collaboration | Varies | Varies | Requires industry partner; can be standalone or supplement |
| **Ideas Lab** | Intensive 5-day workshop producing collaborative proposals | Varies (up to ~$3.75M/yr for 3 yrs in some) | Varies | NSF-organized immersive workshop format |
| **FASED** | Facilitation for scientists/engineers with disabilities | Supplemental | Added to existing | Equipment/assistance to remove barriers |

### 2.2 Other Proposal Types

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Planning** | Support for planning activities leading to future proposals |
| **Conference** | Bringing experts together for research/education discussion |
| **Equipment** | Acquisition of research equipment |
| **Travel** | Travel support for research activities |
| **Center** | Large-scale center proposals |
| **Research Infrastructure** | Building/maintaining research infrastructure |

| **RET** (Research Experiences for Teachers) | Summer research for K-14 educators |
| **IRES** (International Research Experiences for Students) | International collaborative research for US students |
| **INTERN** | Supplemental funding for graduate students to gain non-academic research experience |

## 6. Key Eligibility Notes

- **Who can be PI:** Scientists, engineers, educators at eligible organizations (universities, nonprofits, etc.)
- **MIT is eligible** for all standard NSF programs
- **CAREER:** Must be tenure-track assistant professor with 50%+ appointment; one submission per annual competition
- **SCH:** PI can be on at most 2 proposals per deadline
- **EAGER/RAPID:** Must contact program officer before submitting
- **SBIR/STTR:** Must be a small business (not directly for universities)
- **EPSCoR:** MIT is NOT in an EPSCoR-eligible jurisdiction (Massachusetts is excluded)

---

## 7. How to Find & Apply

1. **NSF Funding Search:** https://www.nsf.gov/funding — filter by directorate/keyword
2. **Grants.gov:** All NSF opportunities also posted here
3. **Research.gov:** Proposal submission and award management portal
4. **Dear Colleague Letters (DCLs):** Watch for special topic announcements
5. **Program Officers:** Contact them before submitting EAGER/RAPID; generally helpful for scoping any proposal
